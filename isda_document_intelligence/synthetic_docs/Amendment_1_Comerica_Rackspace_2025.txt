FIRST AMENDMENT
to the
ISDA MASTER AGREEMENT

dated as of January 15, 2025

between

COMERICA BANK
("Party A")

and

RACKSPACE US, INC.
("Party B")

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Party A and Party B are parties to an ISDA Master Agreement dated as of 
September 26, 2007, including the Schedule thereto (the "Agreement");

WHEREAS, the parties now desire to amend certain provisions of the Agreement to 
reflect changes in the parties' credit profiles and risk management requirements 
following the recent corporate restructuring of Party B;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual agreements contained herein and for 
other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are 
hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE I
AMENDMENTS TO CROSS DEFAULT PROVISIONS

Section 1.1 Cross Default Threshold Amount

Part 1(f) of the Schedule to the Agreement is hereby amended and restated in its 
entirety to read as follows:

"(f) Cross Default. The provisions of Section 5(a)(vi) will apply to Party A and 
     will apply to Party B.

     'Specified Indebtedness' means any obligation (whether present or future, 
     contingent or otherwise, as principal or surety or otherwise) in respect of 
     borrowed money.

     'Threshold Amount' means:

     (i) With respect to Party A: USD 15,000,000 (Fifteen Million United States 
         Dollars), increased from the original amount of USD 5,000,000.

     (ii) With respect to Party B: USD 7,500,000 (Seven Million Five Hundred 
          Thousand United States Dollars), increased from the original amount 
          of USD 5,000,000.

     'Specified Entities' means:

     (i) With respect to Party A: Comerica Incorporated and any Material 
         Subsidiary (as defined in Comerica Incorporated's credit agreement).

     (ii) With respect to Party B: Rackspace Technology, Inc. (the parent 
          company) and any subsidiary that accounts for more than 10% of 
          consolidated revenues."

Section 1.2 Grace Period for Cross Default

The following sentence is added to Section 5(a)(vi) of the Agreement:

"Notwithstanding the foregoing, a Cross Default Event shall not occur unless 
such default, event of default or similar condition or event continues unremedied 
for a period of five (5) Local Business Days after written notice thereof is 
given by the Non-defaulting Party to the Defaulting Party."

ARTICLE II
AUTOMATIC EARLY TERMINATION

Section 2.1 Modification of Automatic Early Termination

Part 1(h) of the Schedule to the Agreement is hereby amended and restated to 
read as follows:

"(h) Automatic Early Termination. The 'Automatic Early Termination' provision 
     of Section 6(a) will apply to Party A and will apply to Party B.

     Notwithstanding Section 6(a), Automatic Early Termination shall only be 
     triggered upon the occurrence of an Event of Default under Section 5(a)(vii) 
     (Bankruptcy) and shall not apply to any other Event of Default."

ARTICLE III
ADDITIONAL TERMINATION EVENTS

Section 3.1 Additional Termination Event

The following provision is hereby added to Part 1(k) of the Schedule:

"(k) Additional Termination Event. The following shall constitute an Additional 
     Termination Event:

     (i) Material Adverse Change. If Party B experiences a Material Adverse 
         Change (as defined below), such event shall constitute an Additional 
         Termination Event with respect to Party B as the sole Affected Party.

         'Material Adverse Change' means any change, event, or circumstance that 
         has or would reasonably be expected to have a material adverse effect on:
         (A) the business, assets, operations, or financial condition of Party B; or
         (B) the ability of Party B to perform its obligations under this Agreement.

     (ii) Change of Control. If a Change of Control (as defined below) occurs 
          with respect to Party B, such event shall constitute an Additional 
          Termination Event with respect to Party B as the sole Affected Party.

          'Change of Control' means:
          (A) Any person or group acquires beneficial ownership of more than 50% 
              of the voting securities of Party B or Rackspace Technology, Inc.; or
          (B) Party B or Rackspace Technology, Inc. merges or consolidates with 
              another entity, unless the existing shareholders retain more than 
              50% of the voting power of the surviving entity."

ARTICLE IV
SET-OFF RIGHTS

Section 4.1 Enhanced Set-Off Rights

Part 6 of the Schedule to the Agreement is hereby amended to add the following:

"Set-off. Upon the occurrence of an Early Termination Date, in addition to and 
not in limitation of any other rights that a party may have under applicable law, 
a party ('X') shall have the right to set off any amounts owed by the other party 
('Y') to X against any amounts owed by X to Y (whether or not arising under this 
Agreement, matured or contingent, and regardless of the currency in which such 
amounts are denominated).

For purposes of effecting any such set-off, X may convert any obligation to a 
single currency selected by X, using rates of exchange determined by X in a 
commercially reasonable manner."

ARTICLE V
REPRESENTATIONS, WARRANTIES AND EFFECTIVENESS

Section 5.1 Representations and Warranties

Each party represents and warrants to the other that:

(a) It has all necessary power and authority to execute and deliver this First 
    Amendment and to perform its obligations hereunder;

(b) This First Amendment has been duly authorized, executed and delivered by it 
    and constitutes its legal, valid and binding obligation;

(c) No Event of Default, Potential Event of Default, or Termination Event has 
    occurred and is continuing;

(d) All representations and warranties made by it in the Agreement are true and 
    correct as of the date hereof.

Section 5.2 Effectiveness

This First Amendment shall become effective as of January 15, 2025 (the "First 
Amendment Effective Date").

Section 5.3 Continuity of Agreement

Except as expressly amended hereby, the Agreement shall continue in full force 
and effect in accordance with its terms. On and after the First Amendment 
Effective Date, each reference in the Agreement to "this Agreement," "hereunder," 
"hereof," or words of like import shall mean and be a reference to the Agreement 
as amended by this First Amendment.

Section 5.4 Amendment Hierarchy

In the event of any conflict between the terms of the original Agreement dated 
September 26, 2007 and this First Amendment, this First Amendment shall prevail.

Section 5.5 Governing Law

This First Amendment shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the 
laws of the State of Michigan (consistent with the original Agreement).

Section 5.6 Counterparts

This First Amendment may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be 
deemed an original.
